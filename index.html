About

I am an incoming postdoctoral researcher at the Hasso Plattner Institute of the University of Potsdam. I am currently finishing my Ph.D. in Social Data Science at the University of Oxford, where I am supervised by Scott Hale and Ralph Schroeder. I also consult for the World Bank and am affiliated with the Weizenbaum Institute.

Research

I study the societal impact of AI, working at the intersection of machine learning, natural language processing, and computational social science. A central focus of my work is evaluation: building rigorous tests of how AI systems behave in the real world, and questioning the data and methods the field relies on to measure that behavior. My past work in this space looked at online harms and bias, from hate speech detection to biases in large language models, and received an Outstanding Paper Award at ACL.

All my publications can be found on my Google Scholar. My research has appeared in PNAS Nexus, ACL, ICWSM and FAccT and has been covered by Al Jazeera, the Tagesspiegel, the MIT Technology Review and more.

My research is supported by a Global Merit Award from the Shirley Scholars Fund and by the Oxford Internet Institute Research Programme on AI, Government and Politics.
